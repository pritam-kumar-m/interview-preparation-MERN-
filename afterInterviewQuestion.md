# After Interview Questions

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer

1. Can you tell me more about the team I would be working with?
2. What are the biggest challenges the team is currently facing?
3. How does the company support professional development and growth?
4. Can you describe the company culture?
5. What are the next steps in the interview process?

## Salary Expectations

- It's important to research the average salary for the position you're applying for in your location. You can say something like:
  - Based on my research and experience, I would expect a salary in the range of $X to $Y. However, I'm open to discussing this further based on the overall compensation package and benefits.

## Why Should We Hire You?

- This is your opportunity to highlight your unique skills and experiences. Example response:
  - I believe I am a strong fit for this position because of my experience in [relevant skills or projects]. I have a proven track record of [specific achievements], and I am confident that I can bring value to your team by [specific contributions you can make].

## What Are Your Weaknesses?

- Be honest and show improvement efforts. Example response:
  - One area I'm working on is [specific weakness]. I've been taking steps to improve this by [specific actions you're taking], and I've already seen some progress in this area.

## Where Do You See Yourself in 5 Years?

- Talk about career goals and alignment with the company:
  - In 5 years, I see myself growing within the company and taking on more responsibilities as I continue to develop my skills.

## Why Do You Want to Work Here?

- Show motivation and company knowledge:
  - I am impressed by the company's mission and values, and I believe that my skills and experience align well with the role. I am excited about the opportunity to contribute to the company's success.

## Behavioral and Situational Questions (STAR-ready)

### Tell me about a time you faced a challenge at work and how you overcame it
- Structure your answer with Situation, Task, Action, Result.

### How do you handle stress and pressure?
- Explain organization, prioritization, and coping strategies.

### Tell me about working with a difficult team member
- Use STAR; focus on communication and resolution.

### Give an example of meeting a tight deadline
- Highlight prioritization, teamwork, and outcome.

### How do you stay current with industry trends?
- Mention blogs, conferences, courses, and communities.

### Tell me about adapting to significant change
- Show flexibility, learning, and positive outcomes.

### Why are you leaving your current job?
- Be constructive: growth, new challenges, alignment with goals.

### Working with limited resources
- Show creativity, prioritization, and results.

### Handling feedback and criticism
- Show openness, reflection, and improvement.

### Leading a team or project
- Use STAR and emphasize leadership actions and outcomes.

### What do you know about our company?
- Demonstrate research and alignment with mission/values.

### Conflict resolution with a coworker
- Use STAR and focus on communication and resolution.

### Prioritization and time management
- Explain methods, tools, and examples.

### Learning a new skill quickly
- Describe process, resources, and impact.

## Questions You Can Ask the Candidate (Interviewer-side)

- Can you walk me through a recent technical project you led or contributed to? What was your role?
- What was the most difficult bug or incident you handled? How did you diagnose and resolve it?
- How do you approach designing a new feature from requirements to release?
- How do you ensure code quality and maintainability in your work?
- How do you mentor junior engineers or share knowledge with the team?
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a technical decision. How did you handle it?
- How do you measure the success of the features you build?
- What strategies do you use to improve application performance?
- Describe your testing strategy (unit, integration, E2E). Which tools do you prefer?
- How do you handle technical debt and prioritization of refactors?
- What are your favorite front-end/back-end tools or frameworks, and why?
- How do you stay up-to-date with new technologies and decide when to adopt them?

## Misc Tips

- Use the STAR method for behavioral answers.
- Be specific with examples and measurable outcomes.
- Tailor salary expectations based on research and total compensation.
